Role: You are a game developer building an educational side-scroller.
Framework: Vanilla JavaScript, HTML5 Canvas, and CSS.
Optimization: Optimized for iPad/Tablet (Touch only, no keyboard).
1. Core Gameplay & Input:
• Movement: Divide the screen into two invisible touch zones. Touching the left 50% moves the player left; touching the right 50% moves them right.
• Interaction: A large, stylized "INTERACT" button in the bottom-right corner. It should only be clickable/visible when the player is overlapping with an NPC.
• Camera: A horizontal side-scrolling camera that follows the player.
2. Visuals & UI:
• Placeholder Art: Player (Blue Rect), Stone-worker (Green Rect), Fisherman (Orange Rect), Village Elder (White Rect).
• Dialogue Box: A dedicated, non-transparent box at the bottom 20% of the screen.
• The Stone Tablet (HUD): A stone-textured box in the top-right. It starts empty. It must have columns for NAME and DEBT.
3. The Narrative Script:
• Initial State: The player needs a 'Sharp Stone'.
• The Promise: Player interacts with the Stone-worker. Stone-worker: "I’ll give you the stone, but you owe me a fish later. I’ll remember." (Inventory updates: +1 Stone).
• The Task: Player interacts with the Fisherman. Fisherman: "Here is your Fish. Go settle your debt." (Inventory updates: +1 Fish).
• The Dispute: Player returns to Stone-worker. Upon contact, Stone-worker triggers dialogue: "Wait! I remember saying I wanted TWO fish. And that fish is too small. You still owe me!"
• The Elder's Intervention: After the dispute dialogue, the Village Elder walks into the center of the frame from the top background.
• The Solution: Elder: "Stop! There have been too many disputes in our village lately. Nobody’s memory can be trusted. From now on, we use this Stone Tablet."
• The Ledger: The Stone Tablet HUD updates to show: [PLAYER] | [1 FISH].
• The Resolution: Stone-worker looks at HUD. Stone-worker: "The stone does not forget. I see the record. The deal is settled."
4. Technical Requirements:
• No keydown listeners. Use touchstart or mousedown.
• Use requestAnimationFrame for smooth 60fps movement.
• Ensure the canvas handles resizing for different tablet aspect ratios.
