Why We Are Making These ChangesEducational Alignment: The game is now about Debt and Trust rather than just "fetching items." By giving items on credit, we create a social contract that the player must eventually fulfill.Removing Logic Blocks: The player can no longer get "stuck" by having an item that an NPC won't accept. If you have the stone, the Woodcutter will take it because he needs it.Mechanical Freedom: Allowing berry collection at any time prevents "back-tracking fatigue" and makes the world feel more open.The "Social Friction" Climax: The conflict now happens during the Settlement Phase at the Town Center. This makes the "Brawl" feel like a result of failed memory/gaslighting, which is exactly what a ledger solves.Replit Agent Blueprint: The "Credit & Settlement" LogicGoal: Refactor the VillageLedgerGame state machine to support a "Credit-First" narrative where items are traded before debts are settled.1.
